Write-up

Motivation

This visualization explores how California wildfire seasons changed between 2000 and 2024 using NASA MODIS imagery and FIRMS active-fire detections. Combining spatial satellite data with temporal statistics, our visualization aims to help users uncover trends in the occurrence, frequency, and severity of fires in California in the 21st Century.

Design Rationale

We designed a satellite map since our particular focus, wildfires, are spatial events. To encode visual severity of the fires, we applied a sequential color scale (yellow to red) based on the Fire Radiative Power (FRP). For temporal exploration, the year slider allows users to scan through time, while the MODIS layer selector lets users compare true color and false color imagery. The accompanying bar chart summarizes annual data and lets users quickly identify unusually severe years like 2020. To prevent clutter and overplotting, we implemented hover details over the bar chart and the fire dot, detailing the coordinates, date, and intensity metrics. The pulse animation was added to catch the users' eye and invite interaction.
